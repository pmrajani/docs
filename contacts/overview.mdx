Contacts are the people your team communicates with on WhatsApp. Every conversation is linked to a contact record.

## How contacts are created

Contacts are created in two ways:

- **Automatically** — when an unknown number sends a message to your WhatsApp number, WapNotify creates a contact using their phone number. You can fill in the rest of the details later.
- **Manually** — click **New contact** in the Contacts section and fill in the details directly.
- **Via CSV import** — upload a spreadsheet of contacts at once. See [import contacts](/contacts/import-contacts).

## Contact fields

Each contact record has the following fields:

| Field | Notes |
|---|---|
| **Name** | Display name shown in the inbox |
| **Phone** | WhatsApp-registered mobile number. Must be unique per workspace. |
| **Email** | Optional |
| **Company** | Optional |
| **City** | Optional |
| **Tags** | Color-coded labels for categorizing contacts. See [tags and lists](/contacts/tags-and-lists). |
| **Notes** | Free-text notes about the contact (visible to all agents) |
| **Assigned to** | Agent responsible for this contact |
| **GSTIN** | GST identification number — used in invoice PDFs for quotations |
| **Billing address** | Used in quotation and sales order PDFs |
| **State name / State code** | For GST compliance on invoices |
| **Custom fields** | Any additional fields your Admin has defined. See [custom fields](/contacts/custom-fields). |

## Soft delete

Deleting a contact soft-deletes it — the record is hidden from the contact list but the conversation history is preserved. If the deleted contact messages your number again, WapNotify automatically restores their record.

## Contact and conversation relationship

A contact can have multiple conversations over time. The Contacts profile page shows all their past conversations, notes, custom field values, and associated leads or quotations in one place.

Opening a conversation from the contact profile takes you directly to that WhatsApp thread.
