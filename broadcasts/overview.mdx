A broadcast is a one-time bulk send of a WhatsApp template message to a group of contacts. Use broadcasts for promotions, announcements, reminders, or any outbound campaign.

## How broadcasts work

Because you're sending outside a customer-initiated conversation, Meta requires you to use an **approved template** for every broadcast. Free-form text is not allowed.

WapNotify sends each message individually — one message per contact — so replies come back as individual conversations in your inbox.

## Targeting

When creating a broadcast, choose how to select recipients:

| Target type | Who receives the broadcast |
|---|---|
| **Contact list** | Every contact in a specific list |
| **By tag** | Every contact with one or more selected tags |
| **All contacts** | Every contact in your workspace |

<Warning>
  Sending to **All contacts** can reach a large number of people. Review the recipient count before sending.
</Warning>

## Delivery tracking

WapNotify tracks the status of each message in the broadcast:

| Status | Meaning |
|---|---|
| **Pending** | Queued, not yet sent |
| **Sent** | Delivered to WhatsApp's servers |
| **Delivered** | Delivered to the recipient's device |
| **Read** | Recipient has opened the message |
| **Failed** | Delivery failed (invalid number, blocked, etc.) |

Open a broadcast and go to the **Analytics** tab to see per-contact delivery status and aggregate rates.

## Replies

When a recipient replies to a broadcast message, their reply appears as a conversation in your inbox — assigned based on your normal routing rules. The 24-hour window opens from the moment they reply, so you can respond with free-form messages.

## Broadcast statuses

| Status | Meaning |
|---|---|
| **Draft** | Created but not yet sent |
| **Running** | Currently sending |
| **Completed** | All messages processed |
| **Failed** | Send aborted due to a critical error |
